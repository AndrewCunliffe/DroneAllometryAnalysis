Drone Allometry Experiment: Summary Report
Andrew Cunliffe <andrewmcunliffe@gmail.com>
Generated on 2020-05-04

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
For the drone allometry project, focussing on seasonal biomass peaks, we have sampled 741 individual harvest plots, from 36 sites with 38 individual photogrammetric reconstructions

4247.59 kg of aboveground biomass has been measured.

Sampling was undertaken using 8 types of drone/sensor: 3DR Solo & MAPIR Survey 2 RGB camera, 3DR Solo with Ricoh GR II, DJI Mavic Pro Platinum, DJI Phantom 3 Advanced, DJI Phantom 4, DJI Phantom 4 Advanced, DJI Phantom 4 Advanced + (with integrated display/control device), DJI Phantom 4 Pro.

For the largest taxa (Adenostoma fasciculatum, Adenostoma sparsifolium, Atriplex polycarpa, Ericameria nauseosa, Juniperus monosperma, Launaea arborescens, Pinus edulis and Prosopis velutina), freshly harvested biomass was weighed in the field and representative sub-samples were then dried to determine the moisture content for each partition (Cunliffe et al., 2020).

In a few instances where plot infrastructure (e.g., posts or flags) were visible in the point cloud (n=20 plots), these points were manually assigned to a noise class and excluded from canopy height calculations.

On average (across all harvest plots), surveys were conducted 94.0 minutes from solar noon. The maximum gap between the survey and solar noon was 258.0 minutes.

Across peak biomass surveys, the mean wind speed was 3.08 m s-1 (ranging from 0.9 to 7.0 m s-1).
